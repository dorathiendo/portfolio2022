Kodak Moments

KodakMoments.com is a website where customers can create unique, personalized photo gifts for their loved ones. As the lead Front-end engineer, I was responsible for developing and maintaining all aspects of the website, including page layouts, product creation tools, and shopping carts. Displayed below is the flow to create a canvas print. The user uploads a photo and is taken to screen where the photo can be edited and previewed on the product. Visit this website at kodakmoments.com.

The Home Page of the Kodak Moments. The user first enters the website here and is show the latest discounts and featured products.

The Product Description Page of customized Canvas Prints. Users can see a sample of the quality of the products and can choose a size to create a print with.

The Upload page allows users to upload their desired images to the placed on the product. Users can upload as many photos as they want, and all uploaded images be saved for use on other products.

The Editing page is where users can crop, rotate, or apply filters to their photos.

The Preview page is where users can view a 3D preview of their product and confirm their changes before adding it to their cart.

Once the user adds their product to their cart, they are redirected to the Cart page. They can continue to create more products and add it to their Cart. When they are ready to purchase, they can return to this page by clicking the Card icon in the top right of the page, and continue to checkout.

Grappling In-House Leagues

Grappling-InHouse.com is a social media website that lets recreational Grappling gyms create leagues, teams, and tournaments. It is a online community that brings together casual fighters who enjoy participating in competitive atmosphere. Since the intended users were had various backgrounds, I wanted to ensure there was simple and easy navigation. I wanted the design to look clean and simple to be unintimidating to users who were used to analog organization of these leagues. Visit this website at grappling-inhouse.com.

The Home page of the website. It lists the upcoming tournaments nearby, and global news from the Brazillian Jiu Jitsu community.

The Login page, where users can enter either their username or email to login. The full Sign Up form is available on the page to allow users to quickly create an account.

The Gyms page allows users to see all participating Gyms at a high level. Each tile links to the gym’s profile page.

The User Profile page display information about a user, including the gyms they are associated with and the history of the tournaments they participated in. The intention is to make this a social media page, that allows users to express themselves and connect with their friends.

Dark Mode was created to account night time use that is easier on the users’ eyes.

Film Dev App

In 2016, I began developing traditional photographic film in my bathroom at home. Initially, I had ruined many rolls of film, mainly because I had difficulty remembering the steps and timing required to accurately develop film. I created the Film Dev App to assist me in the process by listing the steps required for the type of film I want to develop, starting a timer at each step, and indicating when it was time for me to take action. I have designed and developed many iterations of this app, and each time I used the app to develop my film, I made notes of what worked well and didn’t. Elements were chosen for ease of use with one hand, since the app was typically used while dealing with the film and film chemicals. Use this app at film-dev-app.herokuapp.com.
